Our Speakers

Dr. Abdul Quaiyum Ansari

(Professor, Department of Electrical Engineering, Jamia Millia Islamia, India)

Dr. Abdul Quaiyum Ansari is a Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering at Jamia Millia Islamia, New Delhi. He holds a B.Sc. (Engg.) from Aligarh Muslim University, M.Tech. from IIT Delhi, and a Ph.D. in Hierarchical Fuzzy Control. He has held academic positions at King Khalid University (KSA) and Jamia Hamdard, New Delhi. With a strong research portfolio, he has published around 100 international journal papers and 130 conference publications, along with books and book chapters. He has delivered over 150 keynote and invited talks at national and international platforms. His research interests include Computer Networks, Cyber Security, Microwave Antennas, HEMT Devices, and Soft Computing. He is a Fellow and Chartered Engineer of IEI and IETE, and a Senior Member of IEEE. He has held key leadership roles, including Chairman of IEEE Delhi Section (2023–24), and contributes actively to IEEE humanitarian initiatives. He is an IEEE DataPort Ambassador and serves on its Steering Committee. He is a recipient of the ISTE National Award for Promising Engineering Teacher. Known for academic leadership and excellence in teaching, he continues to contribute significantly to research, innovation, and engineering education.

Prof. Dag Øivind Madsen

(Professor, Department of Business, Marketing and Law, USN School of Business, Norway.)

Prof. Dag Øivind Madsen is a Professor at the University of South-Eastern Norway, School of Business, recognized for integrating rigorous scholarship with impactful teaching and academic leadership. With a multidisciplinary foundation spanning accounting, finance, sociology, and organizational psychology, he brings a comprehensive perspective to business education. He holds a Ph.D. from the Norwegian School of Economics (NHH) and an MSc in Accounting and Finance from the London School of Economics. His academic journey is further enriched by studies at the University of Bergen in sociology and work and organizational psychology. His work bridges theory and practice across finance, accounting, organizational behavior, and governance. He is widely regarded for mentoring students and collaborating with industry to translate research into real-world impact. Prof. Madsen continues to contribute meaningfully to advancing business scholarship and practice.

Prof. Muhammad Abulaish

(Professor, Department of Computer Science, South Asian University, India)

Prof. M. Abulaish is a Professor of Computer Science at South Asian University, New Delhi, with over 26 years of distinguished experience in academia and research. He has been serving at SAU since 2016 and has held several key leadership roles, including Chairperson of the Department of Computer Science, Director (Admissions and Examinations), and Acting Registrar. Prior to this, he served for over 18 years as Professor and Head of the Department of Computer Science at Jamia Millia Islamia. He also led the Internet Surveillance and Forensics research group at King Saud University, Saudi Arabia. He earned his Ph.D. in Computer Science from IIT Delhi and founded the Laboratory for Data Science and Analytics (LDSA) at SAU. His research focuses on data mining, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and network analysis, addressing challenges in text mining, social networks, sentiment analysis, cybersecurity, and health informatics. He has published over 140 research articles in reputed journals, books, and conferences, including multiple IEEE/ACM Transactions. He actively contributes to the global research community as an Associate Editor, program committee member for top-tier conferences, and reviewer for leading journals. He holds senior memberships in IEEE, ACM, and CSI, along with lifetime memberships in ISTE, IETE, and ISCA. Through his work, he continues to advance data-driven research with strong societal and industrial impact.

Dr. Iqbal H. Sarker

(Research Fellow, Centre for Securing Digital Futures, Edith Cowan University, Australia)

Dr. Iqbal Hassan Sarker is a research fellow at the Centre for Securing Digital Futures, Edith Cowan University, and an Adjunct Fellow at the DigiSAS Lab, School of Computer Science, University of Technology Sydney. He earned his PhD in Computer Science from Swinburne University of Technology (Melbourne) in 2018. Dr Sarker’s research spans cybersecurity, AI and data-driven technologies, digital twins, and AI applications for business transformation and SMEs. He has published more than 100 peer-reviewed journal and conference papers in outlets such as Elsevier, Springer Nature, IEEE, ACM and Oxford University Press, and is lead author of two Springer Nature research monographs: Context-Aware Machine Learning and Mobile Data Analytics, and AI-Driven Cybersecurity and Threat Intelligence. His research impact has been recognized by ScholarGPS and by inclusion in the world’s top 2% of most-cited scientists (Elsevier/Stanford). He has worked with the Cyber Security Cooperative Research Centre and collaborated with CSIRO’s Data61 through academia–industry partnerships. Dr Sarker teaches and supervises across AI, machine learning, data science, database systems, project management and programming, and contributes to research leadership through editorial boards, conference program committees and postgraduate supervision. He is active in industry engagement and mentoring (including IMNIS) and is a member of ACM, IEEE and the Australian Information Security Association.

Prof. M. N. Hoda

(Director, Bharati Vidyapeeth’s Institute of Computer Applications & Management (BVICAM), India)

Prof. M. N. Hoda is a distinguished academician and technology leader with over 30 years of experience in computer science education, research, and institutional development. He is currently Professor of Computer Science and Founder Director of Bharati Vidyapeeth’s Institute of Computer Applications & Management (BVICAM), New Delhi, a position he has held since 2002. He earned his MCA from Aligarh Muslim University and a Ph.D. in Information System Audit, with research interests spanning Information System Audit, Software Engineering, Artificial Intelligence, Computer Networks, and ICT-enabled pedagogy. He has published over 100 research papers, holds more than 20 patents, and has authored and edited several books and conference volumes. He also serves as the Founder Editor-in-Chief of the International Journal of Information Technology (Springer, SCOPUS indexed). Prof. Hoda has delivered over 250 keynote talks and contributed as a resource person in more than 300 national and international academic events. He has held several prestigious leadership positions, including Chairperson of IEEE Delhi Section and National President (ICT Section) of the Indian Science Congress Association. A Fellow and Senior Member of leading professional bodies such as IEEE, ACM, CSI, and IETE, he has made significant contributions to curriculum development and academic reforms across universities in India. He is also a recipient of the Outstanding Achievement Award and Best IT Teacher Award for his excellence in teaching, research, and institution building.

Dr. Maria Lapina

(Associate Professor, North Caucasus Federal University, Russia)

Dr. Maria Lapina is an Associate Professor of the Department of Computational Mathematics and Cybernetics at North Caucasus Federal University, Stavropol, Russia. She has more than 20 years of teaching experience. She received a PhD in mathematics from Stavropol State University in 2005. She defended her master's thesis in Information Security at the North Caucasus Federal University with honors in 2015. Her research interests include Data Science, Machine Learning, Big Data, Cybersecurity, Cryptography, Trusted Artificial Intelligence, Edu-Tech, Teacher Education, & Higher Ed. She has published in peer-reviewed journals indexed in Scopus and WoS. She has more than 80 research papers. She is an active editorial board member for Springer Nature, IGI Global. She is an active reviewer of MDPI publishing journals. She is the organizer, co-chair and editor of the proceedings of international scientific events: AISMA-2021, 2022, 2023, 2024: International Workshop on Advanced in Information Security Management and Applications, DMAI-2021: International Workshop on Data Mining and AI. She has participated in international internships at Sapienza University of Rome (Italy) in 2018, the University of Ljubljana (Slovenia) in 2018, the Technical University of Dresden (Germany) in 2019 and the 2nd ASEF Innovation Laboratory of Higher Education (ASEFInnoLab) at Fudan University in Shanghai, China in 2020.

Dr. Shahid Tufail

(Senior Data Scientist, NextNRG LLC, Florida, United States of America)
Title of the Talk:
Building Telemetry Pipelines for Infrastructure and Performance Monitoring of Fabric Interconnects in Frontier Supercomputing

Dr. Shahid Tufail is a Data Architect and Technology Lead with over 20 years of experience in designing large-scale data platforms and real-time analytics systems. He specializes in telemetry data streaming, distributed data architectures, and high-performance data engineering across both cloud and on-premise environments. At Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE), he has played a key role in developing high-velocity observability platforms for HPC interconnects, including contributions to the Frontier supercomputer ecosystem. He has been instrumental in designing telemetry pipelines capable of processing millions of metrics per minute, leveraging technologies such as Telegraf, Kafka, VictoriaMetrics, TimescaleDB, OpenTelemetry, and OpenSearch to enable real-time monitoring and performance analysis.

Mr. Mohammad Sohail

(Senior Specialist Data Engineering, LTIMindtree, United States of America)

Mr. Mohammad Sohail is a Data Architect and Technology Lead with over 20 years of experience in designing large-scale data platforms and real-time analytics systems. An alumnus of Aligarh Muslim University, he graduated in 2001 from the Computer Science Department (CSD). He specializes in telemetry data streaming, distributed data architectures, and high-performance data engineering across both cloud and on-premise environments. At Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE), he played a key role in developing a high-velocity observability platform for HPC interconnects and the Frontier supercomputer. He was instrumental in designing telemetry pipelines capable of processing millions of metrics per minute. His work leverages advanced technologies such as Telegraf, Kafka, VictoriaMetrics, TimescaleDB, OpenTelemetry, and OpenSearch to enable real-time monitoring and performance analysis at scale.

Mr. Mehroz Ali Pasha

(Founder & CEO, AHA Technocrats, India)
Title of the Talk:
From Code to Intelligence: How AI is Redefining Software Development and Industry Expectations

Mr. Mehroz Ali Pasha is the Founder of AHA Technocrats, a Noida-based software development and digital solutions company, and brings with him over 14 years of extensive industry experience. Under his dynamic leadership, the company has successfully delivered a wide range of scalable web applications, SaaS platforms, and enterprise-grade solutions across multiple domains, including the development of the official website for Aligarh Muslim University. He completed his graduation from Aligarh Muslim University, earned his MBA from HBTU Kanpur, and further enhanced his expertise by pursuing a specialized course in Supply Chain Management from IIT Kanpur. With a strong vision for innovation and practical learning, he is actively committed to bridging the gap between academic knowledge and industry requirements. His work particularly emphasizes emerging areas such as AI-driven development, modern software engineering practices, and real-world application of technology solutions.
